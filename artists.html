Tatsuro Yamashita

山下 達郎

Tatsuro Yamashita  

山下 達郎

  • Profile
  • Music

Japanese singer-song writer and record producer. One of the famous figures in city pop. Produced majority of his music during 1970s-1980s. Sometimes considered as the king of the genre.

 

Tatsuro Yamashita is heavily influenced by American music, specifically big band, soil and jazz. This is shown from his first album, Add Some Music To Your Day, containing covers of Beach Boys and other “golden-age” American songs.

 

His popular breakthrough came on 1980 with the release of the Ride On Time Album, title track peaking on #3 on the Oricon singles chart, This track is even used in a TV for Maxwell audio cassettes featuring Tatsuro Yamashita.

Mariya Takeuchi

竹内 まりや

Mariya Takeuchi  

竹内 まりや

  • Profile
  • Music

Japanse singer. Famous for her 1984 track “Plastic Love” which gain a resurgence in recent years. In the 1980s, music publishers outside Japan wouldn’t consider publishing Japanese songs (based on Kunihiko Murai’s experience) which makes Mariya Takeuchi work’s at her golden time limited to Japanese audiences.

 

Was married with Tatsuro Yamashita in 1982. Tasuro Yamashita arranged and produced her songs from the start and was arguably considered the most famous couple in the domestic music industry today.

Haroumi Hosono

細野 晴臣

Haroumi Hosono  

細野 晴臣

  • Profile
  • Music

Japanese singer-song writer and record producer. Arguably the one of the pioneers of the city pop genre with his band Happy End.

 

Happy End’s 1971 album, Kazemachi Roman, describes their “disappointment” how the Tokyo of their childhood was swept away and replaced by a high-tech metropolis. Its lyrics were made poetic (in Japanese) and somehow fit the cadence and rhythm of American-style rock. (Sisario B. 2007)

 

“I grew up with American culture, I even regretted that I wasn’t American” – Haroumi Hosono during Red Bull music Academy in Tokyo 2014

 

Kaze Wo Atsumete from Happy End's Album "Kazemachi Roman" 1971

Keiichi Suzuki

鈴木 慶一

Keiichi Suzuki  

鈴木 慶一

  • Profile
  • Music

Japanese singer-song writer. Was one of the pioneers of the city pop genre when he was still with the Hachimitsu Pie band, which was formed on 1972 and disbanded on 1974. The band’s last release being “sentimental street.”

 

The Hachimitsu Pie Band was “changed” to the Moonriders, the band consists of Hachimitsu Pie members and other musicians, including Keiichi Suzuki’s brother, Hirobumi Suzuki.

 

Was the composer for the game Mother and Mother 2 by Nintendo along with Hirokazu Tanaka and Hiroshi Kanazu (on Mother 2).
