Genres

  • Techno City Pop

The earliest incarnations of city pop are grouped into this subgenre. Songs of this subgenre are heavily inspired from California bands such as Buffalo Springfield and Little feat.

 

This subcategory doesn’t only include been easy listening yacht rock cadence songs however, there are plenty of artists who pushed the limits of this category more exotic electro-lounge style, example being the album Pacific (1978) by Tatsuro Yamashita, Haroumi Hosono and Shigeru Suzuki. Arcand R. and Goldner S. calls this style “if Van Dyke Parks was given free reigns to score the hotel lobby music for a psychedelic resort.”

 

都会 from Taeko Ohnuki's Album "Sunshower" 1977

 

During the golden era of city pop, Japan undergo a societal change. Women started gaining access to higher educations and enter jobs. Despite the notion that women need to start a family and marry early, Cohen E. says “it’s was not frowned upon to have little fun before then”.

 

Music of this subcategory tackles topics that especially relate to woman life, unheard of in much music at the time. Singers like Junko Ohashi and Hitohmi Tohyama brought up topics like one-night stands, the pursuit of men and workings of their bedroom.

 

Further developments in this subcategory includes disco-ready style tracks such as After 5 Clash(1984) by Toshiki Kadomatsu and jazz fusion from artists like T-square and Casiopea. These jazz jams ends up leaving a lasting impression on various video games such as Persona, where the composer Shoji Meguro is influenced by their style.

 

Telephone Number from Junko Ohashi's Album "Magical" 1984

 

Before city-pop, Yellow Magic Orchestra used the term techno-pop to describe their synth-heavy grove-oriented dance music style. (Arcand R. and Goldner S. 2019) Despite techno-pop being absorbed into city-pop, the new studio technology that defined this genre became a feature in future city pop.

 

During the economic boom that Japan experience, record labels have more budget for more extravagant recordings (from people buying more CDs, cassettes and tapes to play on Sony Walkman or Betamax VCR). Bringing tools such as Yamaha DX-7, Roland Juno-60, ARP Quadra to the hands of various artists like Tatsuro Yamashita and Toshiki Kadomatsu.
